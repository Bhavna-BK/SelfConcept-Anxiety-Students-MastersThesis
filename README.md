# Relationship Between Self Concept and Anxiety Among Students

## Overview
This repository accompanies my Master’s Thesis: *Relationship Between Self Concept and Anxiety Among Students*  
Published in the International Journal of Creative Research Thoughts (IJCRT), 2023.  

The study explores the relationship between self-concept and anxiety among student populations, highlighting how personal perceptions of self can influence mental health outcomes.

---

## Objectives
- To examine the correlation between self-concept dimensions and levels of anxiety in students.
- To analyze psychological factors contributing to academic and personal stress.
- To provide insights that may inform interventions in educational and clinical settings.

---

## Methodology
- **Design:** Quantitative, correlational study.
- **Participants:** Student sample population.
- **Tools:** Standardized psychological scales for self-concept and anxiety measurement.
- **Analysis:** Statistical methods (e.g., correlation, regression) conducted using SPSS.

---

## Key Findings
- Significant correlations were observed between negative self-concept and higher anxiety levels.
- Positive self-concept was associated with lower anxiety, suggesting a protective role.
- Results emphasize the importance of fostering healthy self-perceptions in educational contexts.
